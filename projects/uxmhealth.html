UX for mHeatlh

Many fantastic mHealth technologies are being made in Scotland, but we don't know enough about how and why people use mHealth technologies and what their impact is in the wild. The UX for mHealth SICSA Research Challenge is bringing together HCI, Data Science, Social Science, Health & Wellbeing and other disciplines to address the design and evaluation of mHealth technology.

What Works in Digital Health?

This two day workshop in July 2015, held in Glasgow, brought together Human Computer Interation, Data Science and Health Behaviour Change researchers to discuss evaluation in digital health. The workshop was co-funded by the Institute for Health & Wellbeing at the University of Glasgow.

The workshop was interdisciplinary, bringing together data science, human computer interaction and health behaviour change. There were three keynote speakers- Dr Neal Lathia (University of Cambridge), Prof Geraldine Fitzpatrick (Vienna University of Technology), and Prof Jeremy Wyatt (University of Leeds). There were 12 short presentations (including speakers from four SICSA departments). There were also three discussion sessions.

Screens in Interaction.

This two day workshop in October 2015, held in Edinburgh, brought together Human Computer Interaction researchers and Sociologists to discuss mobility and UX. The focus was on researching mobile device interaction in the wild.
