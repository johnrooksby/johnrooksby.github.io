About me

I am a researcher in Computer Science at the The University of Glasgow. I moved to Glasgow in October 2012, having held positions at St Andrews and Lancaster. I have a PhD and a first class degree in Computer Science, both from the University of Manchester.

My research is at the intersection of ethnography, design, development. I am interested in how technologies are used in everyday life, and how designers and developers can better support real-world practices. I've also done a number of studies of software developers at work. The fields in which I work include Human Computer Interaction, Computer Supported Cooperative Work, and Software Engineering.

Projects

UX for mHealth

During 2015 I will lead several events on the theme UX for mHealth. This project is funded by SICSA (Scottish Informatics and Computer Science Alliance).

ADUP

I am a co-investigator on The ADUP (Mobile App Data as a Utility for Public Health) project. This is a qualitative study addressing what data is being logged from mHealth applications, and whether and how this could become a resource for public health research. I am working with Marilyn Lennon and Cindy Gray. This research is funded by the RCUK Digital Economy ITaaU Network+.

Populations Programme

I'm a researcher on an EPSRC Programme called Populations: A Population Approach to Ubicomp Systems Design. In this programme we are developing a new science of software structure based upon logging, inference and feedback to developers and users. The principle investigator is Matthew Chalmers.

EuroFIT

I'm a researcher on an EU project called EuroFIT: Social innovation to improve physical activity and sedentary behaviour through elite European football. My work as a part of this project focuses on the development and evaluation of fitness apps.

LSCITS

At St Andrews I worked on the LSCITS Large Scale Complex IT Systems initiative.
